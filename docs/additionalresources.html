Other Training Resources

Research and data analysis

The UBC Library Research Commons offers a variety of training workshops on data analysis and visualization, GIS, research data and citation management, and various software and online tools, including R, Python, and GitHub.

Coursera is an online training resource that can be an option for additional learning. Some suggested courses include:

  • Python for Everybody

  • Bayesian Statistics

Research Ethics

If you are planning to conduct research involving animals, including fieldwork, you will need to undergo the CCAC Experimental Animal User Training Program before applying for research ethics approval.

Similarly, if you will be conducting research involving humans, including surveys/questionnaires, interviews, or workshops, you will need to do the TCPS 2 Course on Research Ethics tutorial before applying for research ethics approval.

Decision Science

The US Fish and Wildlife Service offers both online resources and in-person or online courses on various topics, including Decision Analysis. These are open to others outside the US Dept of the Interior, but note that the fees for in-person training can be expensive for external learners.

Compass Resource Management also has a website on Structured Decision Making

Teaching and Learning

The UBC Centre for Teaching, Learning, and Technology offers Instructional Skills Workshops for Grads and Postdocs, along with other teaching and learning programs and resources

Communication and Facilitation

Compass has some training resources on how to use the Message Box to help better communicate our science to different audiences.

Other professional development training

The UBC Graduate School and the Postdoctoral Fellows Office offers professional development training opportunities throughout the year.

Staff also have access to various learning resources from UBC Human Resources, and may be eligible for some professional development funding.

Many academic societies, such as the Society for Conservation Biology, the Canadian Society for Ecology and Evolution, and the Ecological Society of America, offer online workshops or webinars for members. Information on upcoming workshops or webinars are often sent by email to society members, and may also be available on their websites. In-person workshops and short courses are also offered as part of the annual meetings/conferences of these societies.

Additional Resources

Research and data analysis

The UBC Library Research Commons offers a variety of [training workshops] (https://researchcommons.library.ubc.ca/workshops/) on data analysis and visualization, GIS, research data and citation management, and various software and online tools, including R, Python, and GitHub.

Coursera is an online training resource that can be an option for additional learning. Some suggested courses include: * Python for Everybody (https://www.coursera.org/specializations/python) * Bayesian Statistics (https://www.coursera.org/learn/bayesian-statistics)

Research Ethics

If you are planning to conduct research involving animals, including fieldwork, you will need to undergo the [CCAC Experimental Animal User Training Program] (https://animalcare.ubc.ca/training) before applying for research ethics approval.

Similarly, if you will be conducting research involving humans, including surveys/questionnaires, interviews, or workshops, you will need to do the [TCPS 2 Course on Research Ethics tutorial] (https://tcps2core.ca/welcome) before applying for research ethics approval.

Decision Science

The US Fish and Wildlife Service offers both [online resources] (https://www.fws.gov/training/learning-resources) and in-person [training] (https://www.fws.gov/training) on various topics, including Decision Analysis.

[Compass Resource Management] (https://compassrm.com/) also offers some online resources on [Structured Decision Making] (https://www.structureddecisionmaking.org/)

Teaching and Learning

The UBC Centre for Teaching, Learning, and Technology offers [Instructional Skills Workshops for Grads and Postdocs] (https://ctlt.ubc.ca/programs/all-our-programs/instructional-skills-workshops/), along with other teaching and learning [programs] (https://ctlt.ubc.ca/programs/graduate-student-ta-programs/) and resources (https://ctlt.ubc.ca/resources/)

Communication and Facilitation

[Compass] (https://www.compassscicomm.org/) has some training resources on how to use the Message Box (https://www.compassscicomm.org/leadership-development/the-message-box/) to help better communicate our science to different audiences.

Other professional development training

The [UBC Graduate School] (https://www.grad.ubc.ca/current-students/professional-development) and the [Postdoctoral Fellows Office] (https://www.postdocs.ubc.ca/professional-development-events) offers professional development training opportunities throughout the year.

Staff also have access to various learning resources from [UBC Human Resources] (https://hr.ubc.ca/career-development/workplace-learning-opportunities), and may be eligible for some [professional development funding] (https://hr.ubc.ca/career-development/professional-development-funding).

Many academic societies, such as the [Society for Conservation Biology] (https://conbio.org/professional-development/), the [Canadian Society for Ecology and Evolution] (https://csee-scee.ca/workshops/), and the [Ecological Society of America] (https://www.esa.org/career-development/professional-development-from-esa/), offer online workshops or webinars for members. Information on upcoming workshops or webinars are often sent by email to society members, and may also be available on their websites. In-person workshops and short courses are also offered as part of the annual meetings/conferences of these societies.
